Espressif esp32 development board.

The esp32 developmentboard is a board like the Arduino. based upon the populair esp32 chip from Espressif It is a feature-rich MCU with integrated Wi-Fi and Bluetooth connectivity for a wide-range of applications.

What is an Esp32

An esp32 is a populair programmable microchip with a wide range of uses. You can see the esp32 as a superiour version of the Arduino R3. Some of the esp32 features are:

  • Robust Design
  • Ultra Low Power Consumtion.
  • High Level Of integration.
  • Hybrid Wi-Fi & Bluetooth Chip.

Specs

Some of the main specs are:

    Device summary

  • Microcontroller Tensilica 32-bit Single-/Dual-core Cpu Xtensa LX6
  • Operating voltage: 3.3V.
  • Input Voltage: 7-12V.
  • Digital I/O Pins (DIO): 25.
  • Analog Input Pins (ADC): 6.
  • Analog Output Pins (DAC): 2.
  • UARTs: 3.
  • SPIs: 2.
  • I2Cs: 3.
  • Flash Memory: 4MB.
  • SRAM: 520KB.
  • Clock Speed: 240Mhz
  • Built-in hall effect sensor.
  • built-in temperature sensor.
  • Wi-Fi: IEEE 802.11 b/g/n/e/i:
  • integrated TR switch, balun, LNA, power amplifier and matching network.
  • WEP or WPA/WPA2 authentication, or open networks.
  • 30 or 36 Pins, depending on the model.
  • Capactive touch.
  • CAN 2.0.
  • RMII.
  • PWM.
  • And many more.

Power

Power to the Esp32 is supplied via the 0n-board USB micro B connector or directly to the "VIN" pin. THe power source is selected automatically.

The device can operate on an external supply of 6 to 20 volts. If using more then 12V the voltage regulator may overheat and damage the device. The recommended range is 7-12 volts.
